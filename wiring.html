Signal Conditioning PCB

57×60 mm
Central routing board that filters encoder signals, distributes I2C, and breaks out all ESP32 connections through a single 9-pin header.
Power input (J6): pin 1 = +3V3 from ESP32, pin 2 = GND, pin 3 = +6V from Buck 1
J1 — Encoder 1 Left Motor
  • 1 +3V3 → encoder VCC
  • 2 GND
  • 3 ENC1_A → R1 10kΩ → C2 1nF → filtered
  • 4 ENC1_B → R2 10kΩ → C3 1nF → filtered
J2 — Encoder 2 Right Motor
  • 1 +3V3 → encoder VCC
  • 2 GND
  • 3 ENC2_A → R3 10kΩ → C6 1nF → filtered
  • 4 ENC2_B → R4 10kΩ → C7 1nF → filtered
J8 — IMU MPU-6500
  • 1 +3V3 → IMU VCC
  • 2 GND
  • 3 I2C_SDA
  • 4 I2C_SCL
  • 5 MPU_INT → R7 4.7kΩ pull-up
J3 — PCA9685 I2C 4-pin
  • 1 +3V3
  • 2 GND
  • 3 I2C_SDA
  • 4 I2C_SCL
J4 — PCA9685 Power 2-pin
  • 1 +6V
  • 2 GND
RC filter cutoff: 1/(2π × 1kΩ × 10nF) ≈ 15.9 kHz — kills motor PWM switching noise

PCA9685 Servo Driver

I2C 0x40
Powered by 6V (V+ rail) and 3.3V (VCC logic). I2C bus from ESP32 via signal conditioning PCB.
ChannelServoDirection
CH0Left knee MG996RNormal (pulse 150–600)
CH1Right knee MG996RReversed (pulse = 750 − normal)
CH2Left hip MG996RNormal
CH3Right hip MG996RReversed (pulse = 750 − normal)
CH4–15Unused—
Pulse map: 0° = 150, 90° = 375, 180° = 600
Sit position: knee 80°, hip −80°  •  Stand position: knee 0°, hip 0°

MPU-6500 IMU

I2C 0x68
GY breakout module plugs into signal PCB J8 (5-pin socket). Interrupt pin routed through 4.7kΩ pull-up to ESP32.

Pitch angle = atan2(ax, az)
