01

Overview

SBR-01 is a two-wheeled self-balancing robot with articulated four-bar leg linkages that let it transition between a seated (66 mm) and standing (220 mm) configuration — a 155 mm stance change. An ESP32 closes the balance loop at 100 Hz; a Raspberry Pi 5 sidecar handles vision and high-level behaviour.

System Architecture

Compute is split across two processors with a strict division of labour: the ESP32 owns everything hard-real-time, the Pi 5 owns everything intelligent. They talk over one UART link.

Why two brains

A balance loop cannot tolerate garbage-collection pauses, camera frame drops or OS scheduling jitter — miss a few 10 ms cycles and the robot is on the floor. The ESP32 runs bare-metal Arduino with nothing else to do; the Pi 5 can run heavyweight vision models without ever endangering balance. The UART link is one-way command, one-way telemetry: even if the Pi crashes completely, the ESP32 keeps balancing.

UART protocol (115200 baud, 8N1)

DirectionMessageFormatMeaning
Pi → ESP32P:STANDliteralBegin stand-up sequence
Pi → ESP32P:SITliteralBegin sit-down sequence
Pi → ESP32M:M:<speed>,<turn>Drive command, mixed into PID output
Pi → ESP32G:G:<KP|KI|KD>,<value>Set a PID gain at runtime
Pi → ESP32E:literalEmergency stop — motors off, sit, reset PID
ESP32 → PiT:T:<pitch>,<mL>,<mR>,<state>Telemetry @ 20 Hz (pitch °, motor PWM, SIT/STD/BAL)

The same telemetry stream feeds the Pi's 3.5″ TFT dashboard (pi/display.py, pygame, 30 FPS) which renders a live pitch trace, motor bars and the robot state. For bench work, the ESP32 USB serial accepts single-key commands: W stand, S sit, P print debug, E e-stop, +/- nudge Kp.

03

Control System

Every 10 milliseconds the ESP32 estimates the robot's pitch, runs a PID controller against vertical, and writes the correction to the wheels. The loop is timed with micros() — never blocked, never skipped.

State machine

SIT

Motors off, legs folded. Waits for a stand request.

⇄
STANDING

Cosine-eased leg interpolation. 200 steps × 10 ms = 2 s.

⇄
BALANCE

PID active. Drive commands mix into the balance output.

The PID is armed at 50% of the stand-up sequence (step 100 of 200) — early enough to catch the robot as the wheels load up, late enough that the legs aren't fighting the controller. A pitch beyond ±45° in BALANCE trips fall detection: motors stop, the robot folds back to seated, and the integrator is cleared.

Attitude estimation — complementary filter

θ = 0.98 · (θ + gy·dt) + 0.02 · atan2(ax, az)
98% integrated gyro (fast, drift-prone) + 2% accelerometer angle (slow, absolute) = stable pitch

SensorMPU-6500 (6-axis)GY-6500 module on PCB socket J5
Pitch rategyro Y / 65.5±500 °/s full scale → LSB = 65.5/°/s
Accel angleatan2(ax, az)Axis convention fixed by IMU mounting orientation

PID controller

u(t) = Kp·e + Ki·∫e·dt + Kd·de/dt   where e = 0° − θ
Output clamped to ±255 (8-bit PWM) · integral clamped to ±100 against wind-up
GainDefaultRole
Kp25.0Primary stiffness — proportional push against tilt
Ki1.0Removes steady-state lean (CoG offset, floor slope)
Kd1.5Damping — resists tilt rate, kills oscillation

All three gains are runtime-tunable over UART (G:KP,27.5) or USB serial without reflashing — essential for tuning on the bench. Drive commands are mixed after the PID: mL = u + speed + turn, mR = u + speed − turn, so steering is just an asymmetric disturbance the balance loop continuously absorbs.

Stand-up trajectory

Leg angles follow a raised-cosine ease — t = (1 − cos(π·step/200))/2 — from the seated stance (hip 160°, knee 20°) to standing (hip 90°, knee 90°) over 2 seconds. Zero velocity at both endpoints means no torque spike at lift-off and no overshoot at full extension. Right-side servos are mirrored (180° − θ) because they're mounted facing the opposite direction.

Power System

A 3S Li-ion pack feeds three rails through protection stages and two external buck converters. Motor power deliberately bypasses the PCB — switching current never crosses the logic board.

Actuators

Two drive motors keep the robot upright; four servos move the legs that change its stance.

Wheel drive

ParameterValueNotes
Motors2× JGB37-52037 mm geared DC, 12 V class run at 11.1 V, hall encoders
Wheels65 mm rubberHub-mounted on motor shafts
EncodersQuadrature, 3V3Hall, ch A/B per motor through PCB RC filters
DriverTB6612FNG dual H-bridgeMOSFET, 1.2 A cont / 3.2 A peak per ch · VM direct to battery · VCC + STBY on +3V3
PWM1 kHz · 8-bit0–255 command range = PID output range

Direction logic: forward = AIN1·BIN1 HIGH, AIN2·BIN2 LOW with PWM on PWMA/PWMB; reverse inverts the pairs; both inputs LOW coasts, both HIGH short-brakes. Unlike the old L298N's bipolar bridge (2–4 V drop), the TB6612FNG's MOSFETs pass nearly the full battery voltage to the motors. The 1 kHz PWM fundamental is what the encoder RC filters (fc ≈ 15.9 kHz) are sized against.

Leg servos

ParameterValueNotes
Servos4× MG996R11 kg·cm, metal gear — hip ×2 + knee ×2
DriverPCA9685 @ 0x4016-ch I²C PWM, 12-bit, 50 Hz servo frequency
Pulse range500–2500 µsMapped 0–180° → ticks = µs × 4096 / 20000
ChannelsCH0–CH3Hip-L, Knee-L, Hip-R, Knee-R
Stancessit 160°/20° · stand 90°/90°Hip/knee; right side mirrored as 180° − θ
LinkageFour-bar, 97 mm armsMirrored pairs + 20T 1:1 spur gear train

The mirrored four-bar geometry keeps the foot pads flat through the whole stance transition — measured drift at full extension is ≤4 mm, so the wheels stay loaded evenly while the body rises 155 mm.

Mechanical Design

The entire structure is 3D-printed in PETG: a two-floor 18×15 cm chassis, mirrored side panels, four leg links and top/bottom plates. Target body mass ~471 g excluding battery.

09

Firmware

SBR control v1.1 — Arduino/ESP32, organised as small single-purpose headers around one non-blocking control loop. No RTOS, no delays, no dynamic allocation in the loop path. v1.1 retargets the firmware to the Rev C PCB: SPI IMU with boot-time gyro calibration, TB6612FNG pin map with GPIO15 boot failsafe, interrupt-driven encoder counting, and derivative-on-measurement PID with D-term filtering.

Module structure

FileResponsibility
control.inoMain 100 Hz loop: state machine, PID, safety rules, gain tuning
config.hEvery pin, gain, threshold and timing constant in one place
sensors.hMPU-6500 SPI driver, boot gyro calibration, complementary-filter pitch
encoders.hInterrupt-driven quadrature counting for both wheel encoders
motors.hTB6612FNG direction logic + LEDC PWM (1 kHz, 8-bit)
servos.hPCA9685 driver, stance mirroring, cosine stand/sit interpolation
comms.hUART/USB command parser + 20 Hz telemetry encoder

Loop structure (every 10 ms)

1  imu_read() → complementary filter → pitch
2  comms_parse() → commands from Pi (UART) + bench (USB)
3  emergency stop? → motors off · sit · reset · return
4  |pitch| > 45° in BALANCE? → fall detected · fold to sit
5  state machine: SIT ⇄ STANDING ⇄ BALANCE (PID + drive mixing)
6  runtime gain adjustment (G: commands, +/− keys)
7  telemetry @ 20 Hz → Pi dashboard + serial monitor

Safety rules are evaluated before the state machine each cycle — an e-stop or fall can never be masked by state logic. The PID integrator and previous-error terms are cleared on every state transition so stale wind-up never kicks the robot when balancing re-engages.

Layered protection: cell-level hardware, board-level circuits, and firmware supervision.

Known issues — Rev C

J6.P5 servo rail error (resolved): the PCA9685 V+ pin was originally wired to +5V in the schematic instead of +6V_SERVO, which would have undervolted the servos exactly when the legs load up. The net has been corrected to +6V_SERVO — re-run DRC and re-export Gerbers after the schematic change.
GPIO15 boot-strap: ESP32 GPIO15 (TB6612FNG BIN2) carries an internal pull-up and is sampled HIGH at boot. Firmware writes it LOW in setup() before the driver is enabled — keep this if the pin map ever changes.
GY-6500 SPI mode: the module's CSB/NCS pin must be held LOW for SPI operation. If the IMU answers on I²C address 0x68 instead, CS wiring is wrong.

Pre-power checklist

  • XL4016-A output measured at 6.00 V before connecting servos
  • XL4016-B output measured at 5.00 V before connecting Pi / ESP32
  • TB6612FNG STBY and VCC on the +3V3 rail — driver enabled, 3.3 V logic levels
  • TB6612FNG VM wired directly to the 11.1 V rail, not through the PCB
  • GY-6500 CSB/NCS confirmed LOW (SPI mode active)
  • Motor GPIOs LOW in setup() before the TB6612FNG drives the motors
  • PCA9685 V+ measures 6 V (J6.P5 on the +6V_SERVO rail)
