Overview

Key Features

Six integrated subsystems — sensing, control, actuation, power, structure, and vision.

Self-Balancing

PID control at 100 Hz with complementary filter fusing gyroscope and accelerometer data.

⚙

ESP32 Control

Dual-core 240 MHz microcontroller running real-time firmware with deterministic timing.

6-axis inertial measurement unit on I2C with interrupt-driven data-ready at 100 Hz.

Computer Vision

Pi 5 runs YOLOv8n + MediaPipe at 20+ fps. Person following, gesture control, face recognition.

Custom PCB

4-layer board with 40 components and 0 DRC violations. Replaces 40 jumper wires.

Articulated Legs

4-bar linkage with equal 97 mm arms. 66 mm seated, 220 mm standing variable height.

Why equal-length arms?

With two equal 97 mm links, the upper-arm and lower-arm angles always sum to a constant relative to gravity. The foot tracks essentially straight up and down through the travel range. CAD walk-through confirms 4 mm of forward drift at full extension.

This keeps the center of gravity directly above the wheel axle during stand-up, so the PID controller doesn't fight a moving setpoint.

P
Kp = 25.0
Proportional
Immediate response proportional to error. Primary restoring force that pushes the robot back toward vertical.
I
Ki = 1.0
Integral
Accumulates steady-state error over time. Corrects persistent lean from uneven weight distribution.
D
Kd = 1.5
Derivative
Dampens oscillation by opposing the rate of change. Prevents overshoot during rapid corrections.
Balance Loop — 100 Hz
// Complementary filter float pitch = 0.98 * (pitch + gyroY * dt) + 0.02 * atan2(accelX, accelZ); // PID compute float error = targetAngle - pitch; integral += error * dt; float derivative = (error - prevError) / dt; float output = Kp * error + Ki * integral + Kd * derivative; // Motor output output = constrain(output, -255, 255); setMotors(output);
